Originally called iTV at its reveal, the Apple TV allowed users with an Apple iMac, or at least the iTunes program to sync their movies and even stream them, pictures, tv shows, podcasts, and more to watch them on the big screen.

In early 2008 Apple released a software update that allowed the Apple TV to stream content like movies and tv shows, which could also be bought or rented through the device, via the internet.

It ran a modified version of Mac OS X Tiger, and used an interface that nearly mirrored the look of Front Row for Mac OS.
