The Balopticon was like an early 1900s version of what the PowerPoint application does today. It was named after the Bausch and Lomb Optical Company, though many knew it by another name: the Magic Lantern.

They were widely used in schools to project film images across the country, by photographers or families looking to share their film images, and even some traveling salesmen.

The "handles" on the sides are actually part of the film tray, which slides out on either side, to make switching pictures easy.
