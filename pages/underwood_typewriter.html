The most popular typewriter of the pre-WWII period, the Underwood No.5 laid down a standard of quality that other typewriter companies were quick to copy.

It worked by using metal rods with small letter and number stamps on their ends to strike an ink ribbon, and leave marks on an inserted sheet of paper.
